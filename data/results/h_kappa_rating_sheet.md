# H Judge Validation — Blind Human Rating Sheet

Rate each of the six artefacts on the five criteria below, **1 to 5** (integers). Rate each artefact **on its own**, against its brief. Do **not** try to guess which system wrote it, and rate before looking at any model score — the whole point is an independent second opinion to validate the automated judge (Cohen's weighted kappa, threshold kappa >= 0.6).

## Criteria (same five the automated judge uses)

- **completeness** — Conveys the substance the brief asks for, using the table's data.
  _1 = misses most required content; 3 = covers the main points, minor gaps; 5 = fully covers everything the brief asks for._
- **responsiveness** — Reflects the reviewer feedback that was given (if no feedback was given, score 5).
  _1 = ignores the feedback; 3 = partially incorporates it; 5 = fully incorporates every requested change (or no feedback was given)._
- **coherence** — Logical organization and structure appropriate to the format.
  _1 = disorganized/hard to follow; 3 = readable, some flow issues; 5 = clear, well-structured for the format._
- **tone_audience** — Matches the required tonality and suits the stated audience.
  _1 = wrong tone/register for the audience; 3 = acceptable but uneven; 5 = pitch-perfect for tone and audience._
- **conciseness** — Free of padding and redundancy; tight for its purpose. Do NOT reward length.
  _1 = padded/repetitive; 3 = mostly tight, some filler; 5 = no wasted words._

> Scoring tip: 3 is "adequate / acceptable", 4 is "good", 5 is "excellent". Reserve 1–2 for real problems. Conciseness is NOT about length alone — a short artefact can still be padded, a longer one tight.

```
## Summary
Q3 2025 combined top-line growth with improved unit economics. Revenue rose to EUR 2,530,000 (from EUR 2,250,000 in Q2), while gross margin improved to 73% (from 71%). Customer momentum strengthened with 610 new customers and a lower churn rate of 2.7%. Win: simultaneous growth and margin expansion. Risk: sustaining acquisition volume without degrading cohort quality and reversing recent retention gains.

## Financials
Revenue of EUR 2,530,000 was EUR 280,000 higher than Q2, a 12.4% quarter-over-quarter increase. Gross margin increased by 2 percentage points to 73%, supporting stronger contribution per euro of revenue. Key watchpoint is maintaining margin at 73% as volumes scale, particularly if go-to-market tactics increase discounting or cost-to-serve.

## Customers
New customers increased from 480 in Q2 to 610 in Q3 (+130). Retention improved, with churn declining from 3.1% to 2.7%. This reduces replacement demand and makes revenue gains more durable. The primary risk is that rapid growth can introduce less-fit segments; monitoring churn by cohort will be important to ensure it remains at or below 2.7%.

## Outlook
For Q4, priorities are to protect gross margin at 73%, sustain acquisition performance (610 new customers in Q3 as the baseline), and hold churn at 2.7%. Execution focus should remain on disciplined customer targeting, onboarding that supports retention, and cost management to avoid margin slippage while pursuing growth.
```
